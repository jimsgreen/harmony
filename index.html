Grade 5 Math Quiz

Practice Strategies and Skills

Time: 0s

Properties of Operations (Strategy: Using Properties)

1. Which property does this equation show? (7 + 3) + 2 = 7 + (3 + 2)

2. Explain how you can use the Distributive Property to solve 6 × 14 in your own words.

Addition, Subtraction, and Estimation (Skill: Estimation)

3. Estimate: 498 + 302 ≈ ?

4. Show how you can check subtraction by using addition.

Multiply Whole Numbers (Skill: Multiplication)

5. What is 36 × 24?

6. Write a multiplication story problem with whole numbers and solve it.

Divide and Estimate Quotients (with 2-Digit Divisors) (Skill: Division)

7. Estimate: 742 ÷ 18 ≈ ?

8. Show how you would estimate 945 ÷ 22. Explain your steps.

Adjust Quotients (Skill: Division Adjustment)

9. A school has 1,005 pencils to give to 42 students. About how many pencils will each student get?

10. Explain how you would adjust your quotient if you divide 873 by 29 and your first guess is too high.

Division Strategies (Skill: Strategy Use)

11. Which is a good way to divide 96 ÷ 8?

12. Write one strategy you can use to solve 144 ÷ 12.

Whole Number Operations Review (Skill: Mixed Practice)

13. A theater has 12 rows with 36 seats each. If 278 people bought tickets, how many empty seats are there?

14. Explain how you solved a word problem that used both multiplication and subtraction.

Teacher Mode: Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: Associative Property. (Changing the grouping doesn’t change the sum.)
Q3: 498 ≈ 500, 302 ≈ 300 → 800.
Q5: 36 × 24 = 864.
Q7: 742 ÷ 18 ≈ 40 (since 18×40=720).
Q9: 1,005 ÷ 42 ≈ 24.
Q11: Skip count by 8s is correct.
Q13: 12×36=432 seats. 432–278=154 empty.
