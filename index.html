Q4 (Open). Use long division to solve 7,128 ÷ 24. Show your work and state the quotient.

24 × 300 = 7,200 (too high), try 24 × 297 = 24 × (300 − 3) = 7,200 − 72 = 7,128. So the quotient is 297.

3) Distributive Property of Division

Q5 (MC). Which expression correctly uses the distributive property to compute 84 ÷ 6?

Answer: B
Split 84 into two multiples of 6: 60 and 24. Then 84 ÷ 6 = (60 ÷ 6) + (24 ÷ 6) = 10 + 4 = 14.

Q6 (Open). Use the distributive property to compute 126 ÷ 7. Show your split and your reasoning.

One way: 126 = 70 + 56 (both multiples of 7). Then 126 ÷ 7 = (70 ÷ 7) + (56 ÷ 7) = 10 + 8 = 18.

4) Lesson 2.6 — Divide by 2‑Digit Divisors

Q7 (MC). What is 2,016 ÷ 63?

Answer: C (32)
63 × 32 = 63 × (30 + 2) = 1,890 + 126 = 2,016.

Q8 (Open). Divide using partial quotients: 1,764 ÷ 42. Show each partial chunk you subtract.

Q9 (MC). Estimate 3,189 ÷ 47. Which is the best estimate?

Answer: B (64)
Round 47→50 and 3,189→3,200. Then 3,200 ÷ 50 = 64.

Q10 (Open). Compute 2,375 ÷ 48. A student guessed 50 because 48≈50. Explain how to adjust the quotient and give the final answer with any remainder.

Since 50×48 = 2,400, that’s greater than 2,375, so 50 is too high. Try 49: 49×48 = 2,352; remainder 2,375−2,352 = 23. Final: 49 R 23 (or 49 and 23/48).
